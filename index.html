Assumptions

  1. The Superannuation Guarantee (SG) contribution is at the rate of 9.5% of ordinary time earnings.
  2. The tax rate for taxable income of superannuation in accumulation phase is 15%, including on concessional contributions and income generated.
  3. Members will convert the accumulation balance into pension at the nominated pension start age. All contributions will be converted into the pension at the beginning of the next financial year.
  4. Salaries are indexed at the wage index level if there is any.
  5. Member is able to select the base pension drawdown amount or just withdraw the minimum. If member nominates their own pension drawdown strategy, the annual drawdown will be indexed at CPI level.
  6. Investment earnings are uniformly distributed over the financial year.
  7. SG, pension drawdowns, fees and insurance premiums are received or paid in the middle of each financial year.
  8. Tax is paid at the end of each financial year.
  9. The age pension entitlements are calculated under the current new deeming rules, taking into consideration the income test and asset test. Note the calculation of age pension needs to be altered if the standard/criterial is updated by the Centrelink.
  10. The deeming rate is maintained at the current deeming rate according to the Centrelink website for the entirety of the projected period.
  11. There is no change in personal assets or circumstances.
  12. The age pension supplement is assumed to be at the maximum level if there is any.
